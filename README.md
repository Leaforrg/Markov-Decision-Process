**DESCRIPTION**

Think of a Markov Decision Process (MDP) as the logic behind how an AI makes a series of smart choices in a game. It breaks decision-making down into four simple parts:

Agent: The player or character making the choices (like a King in chess).
State (S): The current "snapshot" of the situation (where the piece is on the board).
Action (A): The possible moves you can make (left, right, or forward).
Reward: The "score" or benefit you get for a move (staying alive vs. getting checkmated).

The process is a loop: The Agent looks at its current State, picks an Action that promises the highest Reward, and lands in a New State (S). 
Over time, the AI learns which actions lead to the best long-term wins rather than just quick points (Markov Decision Processes - the Decision Lab, 2021).


Reference: Markov Decision Processes - The Decision Lab. (2021). The Decision Lab. https://thedecisionlab.com/reference-guide/statistics/markov-decision-processes
